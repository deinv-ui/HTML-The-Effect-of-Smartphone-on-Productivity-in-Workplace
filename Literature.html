  • Abdullah Aslam Fraj AlZwamri & Norlaila Hussain Dhofar University Oman
    Title - The impact of smartphones on work productivity as perceived by employees at a government department in Salalah, Oman (2018)

    • The research studies the impacts of smartphones on productivity in a government department, they prepared questionnaires in English and Arabic. The questionnaires were distributed to 120 people and 110 responses were received. The result of the study suggests the usage of smartphones helps in productivity of the employees.

    The study identifies four factors that may affect employee productivity based on literature review. The four factors are smartphone usage, restrictions, satisfaction and mobility. The study found that the employees have a medium level of smartphone usage during work, most of the employees use smartphones in their workplace silently but not during work and meetings. There is a medium level of restriction in smartphone usage in the department, most employees prefer to use their smartphones during breaks, it is to avoid penalties or salary reduction due to excessive uses of smartphones.

    In the aspect of satisfaction, most of the employees are happy and satisfied because they are allowed to keep their smartphone to themselves during work, this allows them to stay connected to their family in case of an emergency. For mobility, the majority of the employees agree that smartphones are a mobile tool to communicate with their colleagues, the smartphones help them in exchanging views and information at any time or any place. Based on the research, the researcher gave some recommendations to improve the productivity of employees of the studied government department. The recommendations include allowing the use of smartphones during work hours, but regulations must be improvised to avoid the misuses of phones. Secondly, develop specialized applications that could be used by the worker in specific tasks in their work. Moreover, develop an application for the employees to communicate with each other and ease the exchange of information and knowledge in work.

    Title - Smartphone addiction, daily interruptions and self-reported productivity (2017)

    • The research studies smartphone addiction, daily interruptions and self-reported productivity in German, they prepared online questionnaires and publicized it on German media. They received 605 responses, and 379 responses met the criteria of the study. The result of the research indicates that smartphone addiction is related to greater level of daily interruptions and lower productivity of work. A negative relationship of smartphone addiction and work hours is found in the research. Furthermore, smartphone usage has a negative relationship with the negative effects on participants' lives.



  • Surya Nair
    Title - Does Smartphone affect productivity? (2021) )

    • The research studies the impacts of smartphones on productivity, they prepared questionnaires in English and Arabic. The questionnaire has received 111 responses. Most of the respondents agree that smartphones have increased their productivity. Most people use smartphones for more than 3 hours daily. The most frequently used applications on smartphones are social media types. More than half of the respondents state that they can perform better without smartphones. The benefits of smartphones are allowing people to expand their knowledge and communicate more easily. The drawbacks of smartphones are reduced face-to-face interactions and increased distractions.
